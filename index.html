Building a More Sustainable Future

Explore Intel’s sustainability milestones and our path to net-zero by 2040.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore form Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

Intel's founding set the stage for microelectronics progress and a long-term commitment to advancing computing in energy-efficient ways.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor.

This innovation launched an era of more efficient computing and opened doors for energy optimization at scale.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor establishing the x86 architecture.

x86 became foundational for many systems, influencing power and performance approaches for decades.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture.

This enabled new software complexity while prompting advances in energy-efficient design.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations.

Following this, Intel invested heavily in abatement, efficiency and renewable energy to reverse emissions trends.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launch of RISE and 2030 goals for climate, water and waste.

RISE formalized Intel's plan to lead on responsible and sustainable manufacturing worldwide.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Commitment to net-zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2040.

This reflects a bold roadmap of renewable energy, efficiency, and process innovation across operations.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity worldwide.

Widespread renewables greatly reduced operational emissions and accelerated decarbonization.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts a global Sustainability Summit.

The summit brought together partners to scale sustainable semiconductor manufacturing and supply chains.
